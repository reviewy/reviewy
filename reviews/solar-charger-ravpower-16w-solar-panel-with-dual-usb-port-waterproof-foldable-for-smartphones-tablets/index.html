You will get a charge out of this!

This is a FINE charger for people who love the outdoors and are friendly and respectful to their environment. The solar panels and two USB charging ports are well protected by a heavy duty nylon case which fastens securely with integral Velcro strips. I tested it with two devices charging simultaneously, a Motorola Tracfone and a Creative Labs VADO Cam video camera. They both charged fine on a gray, typical, Pacific Northwest fall day*, with only occasional sun breaks. The pictures show my test setup. I also tested it with my son’s Samsung Galaxy 3 smartphone and it charged it from 60% to 75% in about an hour on a very overcast day where the sun was only visible as a bright area in a very gray, overcast sky. The charging unit folds up to about 10” x 7-1⁄2” x 1” and would easily fit in a small day pack for taking along with you on the trails ahead. Note that it does not come with any charging cords or adapters and you would need one with a standard USB connector for your devices you plan to charge with it.*The 2 photos were taken on November 18th 2014, in my home which is about 90 miles North of Seattle Washington. The weather on this day at around 11:00am was 44 degrees and partly cloudy.

Fast & Durable Waterproof Solar Charger

This is a compact, fold-able solar charger that powers itself solely from the sun. It is very well made with heavy duty rugged nylon canvas material, Velcro straps & stainless steel eye-lets. The RAVPower 16W Dual USB Solar Charger has 3 separate solar panels and it does NOT have any capacity to store. It’s a straight to the device(s) charger (or to an external battery pack). All the power comes direct from the sun, which outputs to the dual USB ports (utilizing iSmart technology that adjusts to connected device’s power draw) that will charge up to two USB devices simultaneously. It works in sunny and cloudy climate but is not for use in inclement rainy weather.Comes in a durable & waterproof nylon fabric booklet, with four carabiner hooks, two flat Micro USB cables (one short, one standard length) and an instruction manual.I use this during my field work as a Land Surveyor where carrying external power banks are not ideal or where there are no wall sockets in sight. Output is fairly quick for a solar charger (see the screenshot of my phone while charging), but not as fast as my Quick Charge chargers which was expected. It will charge your device at it’s fastest in direct sunlight. In cloudy days with little sun, you’ll see a reduction in charging speed. I see the RAVPower 16W Dual USB Solar Charger ideally for use while hiking, camping, at the beach, bicycling or any fun/work environment were power isn’t an option.One of the things I didn’t like about this solar charger: the handy built-in compartment that stores the two USB ports, can also be used to store one phone while charging it, but I found the fit to be a bit tight (would prefer slightly larger) and would’ve liked the location of compartment to be at the top of the solar panels (where the compartment would be facing up) and not below the solar panels (with opening facing down) when hanging the charger off of it’s hooks. I don’t feel comfortable in the phone staying put (see photos). The compartment is too small for tablets or thick devices. Despite this small caveat, I recommend it.READ MORE
