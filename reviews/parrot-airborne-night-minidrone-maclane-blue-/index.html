I was really excited about this product and had one great indoor flight, then recharged. Two days later - a software update occurred, but I thought nothing of it and went outside. After hitting the “take off” button, I saw the drone climb and stopped responding to my control inputs. I hit the “emergency” button to make the drone land, but there was no response… It kept climbing until it was out of sight. Apparently this uncontrolled ascent is a common issue looking at other reviews and Parrot’s online support forums.I have contacted Parrot’s customer service line and will adjust my rating if they respond well, other wise - know that I spent $130 on a drone for 10 minutes of flight time.

The best INDOOR drone on the market today!

This should be marketed as an indoor drone; if Parrot had done that, this quadcopter would have had 5 star reviews.I own a dozen different drones - from a Cheerson mini to a Phantom 4, Chroma 4k, Typhoon 500+, and Typhoon H.This is the ONLY mini drone that I know of that has a sonic sensor to maintain altitude and position. Sure the Typhoon H, Phantom 4 and 3 Pro have altitude sensors for indoor use, but they are OUTDOOR drones…where they work best with a GPS signal.This Parrot holds a rather (surprisingly) steady attitude indoors! If I hover in one spot, it stays within a foot (generally) of the area without any input.This would be a great drone to practice your ‘Nose In’ moves.I bought mine for just over $50, and at that price, there is NOTHING on the market that has the INDOOR stability of this drone. This thing is an absolute steal at $50!If you are looking for an indoor drone that is stable and quick, this is it! Nothing else comes close.I do NOT recommend flying a bluetooth drone outdoors because of the signal problems. But indoors, this quad is magic!

Small Drone Big Features

This drone is amazingly stable in flight and very fun to fly for its size it is one of the most advanced drones on the market. I really like the headlight function and the decals on this drone since it usually grabs attention and adds to the experience and since you can fly this drone with your Android or Apple mobile device, tablet, and etc through Parrots app anyone can fly this drone and perform maneuvers like a pro. There are three small things however that I dont like about this drone though 1.Drone works off of Bluetooth and not WiFi 2.Flying the drone on the controller or ace modes can be difficult due to the virtual joysticks on your device 3.Drone comes with one battery instead of two so every 5-8 mins you are looking for a receptacle to charge the drone for about 25 mins. All things considered this is the drone to have if you want a high tech, easy to fly, nicely crafted drone. Also as a side note I had to contact Parrot customer service and they responded quickly to my email and even sent me a part for this drone at no cost even though I bought it as an open box item.
