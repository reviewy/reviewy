Worst smelling Lysol ever!

Worst smelling Lysol ever! I ordered this while out of town because we were all coming home sick after the holidays and I wanted something to kill the flu germs we’d be spreading around our house. I went by the assumption that ‘Baby’s Room’ would be softer scent. What a mistake! Unfortunately, this scent is so overwhelming, I can’t be in the same room it was used in without getting getting nauseous. I’m not typically sensitive to smells, I can use bleach cleaner without the bathroom exhaust fan on and I’m fine, yet THIS Lysol scent makes me gag and gives me a headache. It also lingers, strongly, for a really long time. I went to my local grocery store a few days after receiving this and sniffed the other 4 Lysol variants they had and every single one of them was a lighter and more pleasant smell then this. I bought a can of the original Lysol to replace this because it was more pleasant.

A Classic: It just works + the price is right

Lysol is a classic and nothing beats it for, say, deodorizing indoor pet accidents, to which my senior dog has regrettably become more prone. Also great for any damp spaces prone to mold, like under a sink. I really don’t want to know about the environmental consequences, thank you, although perhaps vinegar and baking soda might be more ecologically righteous. When used sparingly, I seriously doubt I am hastening Climate Change in any significant way–plus it doesn’t take much of the stuff to do the job.When ordered as an “add-on” with Amazon Prime, this large-sized aerosol canister–which I fully expect to be good for six months–is considerably cheaper than the same size from the supermarket.No, the scent is not my absolute favorite, but fortunately it and any offending odors dissipate quickly. Despite the wall-to-wall TV ads for all the new products promising to make housework ecstatic bliss, more and more I find myself returning to the very same classics my mother and grandmother used, neither of whom was inclined to waste either time or money on anything just because it was “new and improved.“IMO, this is a product that should be under everybody’s kitchen sink, along with Borax and Bon-Ami. One place I have yielded to the environmentalists is in drastically reducing my reliance on chlorine beach (aka “Clorax”) which really does do dreadful things to ecosystems when it leaches into the ground water. Actually one so seldom really needs it. For smelly stuff, there’s a light touch of Lysol and for disinfecting, hydrogen peroxide is nearly the equal of bleach and much more gentle on fabrics, plus w/ patience it’s nearly the equal for stain-lifting.
