iRocker Paddle Boards Cruiser, Perfect All Around Paddle Board!

Whether you are out for a relaxing day on the water or tracking miles down the coast, up the river, or across the lake, the iRocker Paddle Boards Cruiser is the perfect all around SUP.  Don’t be surprised with all the looks and attention you get on this amazing looking SUP. This board boasts 10’6” in length, 33” in width, and a 6” thickness.  The 33” width and extra wide tail makes the iRocker Cruiser as stable as an inflatable board comes in any water conditions, whether you are in choppy or flat water.   The deck pad has been extended all the way to the tail of the board for those interested in Paddle Board Yoga, Fishing from your SUP, or you would like to take a cruise with your significant other.   The expanded front bungee area gives 3.3 square feet of storage space, enough room to secure all your gear.  The D-Rings also allow you to strap your gear down so go ahead, pack your lunch box with dinner and catch a perfect sunset out on the water. The bottom of the board has a tri-fin set up with 2 side fins permanently fixed to the board and one larger removable center fin.  The tri-fin set up allows for straight tracking while paddling to keep the side to side paddle motion to a minimum.

iRocker Cruiser vs Sport

This is our second iRocker SUP, the first was the 11’6 Sport version. I wasn’t paying attention to the version and found the sport a little unstable for my 6’3 250lb body. My wife on the other hand had very little problems (5’5 130lbs). I bought the cruiser for her thinking it’ll just take a little time for me to get comfortable on the Sport. Ironically she can use both with little balance issue so it looks like I’m now the proprietor of the Cruiser.We paddleboarded in Jamaica last year on the resort’s big Hobbie SUP’s. It was fun so we wanted to get back in to it. With a hybrid car, midsize SUV, and not a lot of storage in the garage, we decided to get inflatable SUPs. Besides a week in Jamaica last year, we have no other PB experience.On the Sport my wife was up and paddling with no issue outside of the initial couple of wobbly stand ups and sit downs. She’s 5’5ish and has a low center of gravity.On the Sport I was very wobbly to stand up and very non-acrobatic to get back down. Paddling from a kneel or sitting position is no issue. After an hour or so and some forced stand-ups I was able to get up and shakily get going. This inflatable SUP is stable but nowhere as stable as the resorts big Hobbie’s. After an hour or so I was comfortable enough but my legs were burning from the balance board session I had that day. The next time out we got the Cruiser and I used both boards. I was much less wobbly this time out and it was a lot easier to stand and get moving.The difference between the Cruiser and the Sport is not day-and-night but it is noticeable. For me the cruiser is more stable and easier to stand up on. The Sport definitely seems to glide through the water easier and faster. While noticeable, I don’t know that the differences are so stark that you have to buy one over the other for your intended need. They are both great boards. If I had it to do over again (I’m only 3 outings in) I’d probably get the Cruiser but the stability differences are not so drastic that I’m hating my Sport purchase - I just think I could of got up and going quicker and been confidently paddling sooner had I initially bought the Cruiser.This last outing I took the dog with us. A small 22lb Jack Russell. We got a life jacket for our fearless little family member. She rode fine though sometimes her moving around and biting at the wake did “jack” with my balance. She’ll be going with us on future outings. Definitely a lot easier to have her as guest with me while I’m on the Cruiser. On the Sport her movements have a little more effect on our vessel. I’m sure all of this will smooth out over the next couple of outings.On a final note, I think the higher center of gravity for my 6’3 body and the extra 100+lbs makes my initial experience with these inflatable SUPs a lot different than my wife’s. Where I was wobbly and shaky she had minimal wobbles. I have better balance than her on land and in sports. With that said, I have no doubt both of these iRocker paddleboards will bring us a lot of fun and exhausting weekends. If you’re a bigger guy/girl especially if you’re taller, just understand that it’s going to take a few sessions to get your SUP legs but be patient and keep at it. It gets easier each time.
