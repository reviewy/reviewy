It’s not good for much

This is really a “toy” board. It’s not good for much. I bought it because I wanted something cheap to paddle around flat water just to prevent my paddling muscles and core from atrophying when I was out of town for several months. Here are my complaints about it:- This board does not have the shape pictured. It has a pulled in nose. Also, unlike other reviews have stated, if you order the 7’ board, you’ll get the 7 foot board. (The “84” painted on the deck must be referring to the year when this shape of board became popular, and NOT the length, which is 7’.)- The material is not what you would expect for a normal foam board. It’s a kind of sponge-like styrofoam, more like a boogie board, and is very cheaply made.- The name of the company is a misnomer. This is really the “Made in China” board company. (The tip off was on the shipping box.)- It’s too small to work in small waves, and wouldn’t be any good on a wave with a drop as I can’t imagine carving turns on it. It would be like trying to dance in shoes made of Jello.The only use I can see for this board is for a kid (6-12) who wants to learn how to surf. I think I’m going to give it to my nieces to play with when I’m done using it. It might also be okay for someone who really just wants work on their paddling and doesn’t plan to try and surf it. It’s very floaty. But if you are an adult and want to learn how to surf, drop the cash and get a better board–either a better quality foam longboard, or an epoxy board of the same length for getting a feel for the paddling on a shorter board.
