Solid VR at an affordable price

First off, the shipping’s quick: got my headset in two days coast to coast. Usually a good indicator the company means business. Packaged nicely with easy to understand and basic instructions.Build quality is good, and the unit is snugly put together. The headband is stretchy and thick, also done nicely from solid materials. The buttons on the unit for navigation and hardware control are clean and snappy, with intuitive layout. It feels a bit odd at first, but a little tinkering makes you comfortable quick.The headset wears fairly comfortable, considering it’s a head-mounted computer. That being said, after about two hours I felt like I needed just a touch more padding. Other than that, the unit is so well done, with pupillary adjustment and scalable focus, (absolutely necessary after taking it to the bar and letting nearly a dozen friends flip out on it) that I have no complaints on the quality of the object itself. The lenses are easily removable to clean with the included cloth, and the molding surrounding all the interfaces on the bottom front ledge of the unit are cleanly molded with easy access. Headphones plug in snugly and the sound is wonderful. Haven’t filled the local SD yet, so I can’t tell you about the expandable storage. If it’s as easy to handle as the wifi and Bluetooth settings, than it’s completely painless.The unit comes loaded with some great VR experiences and a few good games. There’s currently a plethora of companies hosting VR content on the pre-loaded Google Play Store, so a quick link up to wifi allows you access to damn near anything you can think of VR. It’s friggin’ awesome. The graphical prowess of the unit is nothing to scoff at either. The 2560x1440 5.5 inch screen is gorgeous, refreshing at a headache-free 75Hz. This unit’s so good, it’s waiting for content to catch up with it. After experiencing phone-based VR, then trying these, I can comfortably say the difference is like night and day. Worth the price in my opinion. 4K support, HDMI in, Wifi, Bluetooth, AND Android 5.1 WITH the company saying firmware updates?! Hell, why not? It beats any phone-based VR I’ve seen, and the battery life isn’t bad either. I got nearly 4 hours straight on a %93 charge. And everybody I let demo it now wants one. I’ll update this review after I try the SD, USB, and HDMI ports.
