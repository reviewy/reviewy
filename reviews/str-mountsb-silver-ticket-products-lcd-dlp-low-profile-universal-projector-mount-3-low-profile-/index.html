    Product Description

Size:3” Low Profile The Universal Projector Mount by Silver Ticket Products is a durable, well-designed, adjustable projector mount intended to fit all types of both old and new projectors. Its simple design is intended to provide a stable, safe way for you to mount your projector exactly where you need it. This mount features adjustable extension arms that are compatible with mounting patterns up to 9.625” x 9.625”. The Universal Projector Mount comes in three models, each with different vertical extension heights, so choose the one that is just right for your application. This mount is crafted from high-grade steel for powerful durability at an affordable price.

The Universal Projector Mount includes four extendable, swiveling, spider-type arms for completely universal mounting capability. Easily position your projector with the tool-less tilt-adjustment knob and tool-less washer and thumb-screw vertical extension adjustment. The Universal Projector Mount allows steady, secure placement of any projector from 2.75” to 3”. Full 360 degrees rotation and 15 degrees tilt adjustment allows for perfect alignment of your projector.

Servicing your projector is simple with the tool-less quick-release system. This projector mount is the perfect choice for any setting - whether it is a classroom, boardroom, home theater, or anywhere else you may need to install a projector. Product Reviews

Not sure about those small screws

I’m not quite done installing the projector yet so this is an interim report.I’m installing a Benq HT1085ST projector in the basement with a rather lowish ceiling and absolutely needed a low profile mount. This mount is probably the lowest profile that is going to be physically possible to make that also allows some post-install pitch/roll/yaw adjustments. Watching the youtube instructional video is an absolute must, there’s no way to do the first step correctly without it. Also, one thing not mentioned in the video is that after you’ve got the first step done correctly you should not rotate the top or bottom portion. If you do rotate it the thing comes a bit loose. One set of the supplied screws for fastening to the projector worked for my projector, however the other screws that effectively hold the projector to the mount have a disturbingly short thread (maybe less than 1⁄4 inch) and it is scary to think that these 3 little screws are holding it all up.I managed to find a spot on the ceiling where I can hit the joist for support for 2 screws and thinking of getting toggle bolts for the other two (and not use the supplied wall anchors). I will report back once I’m done hanging it all together.Update: Got it mounted and it is indeed a very low profile mount (see attached picture). After mounting it is a bit hard to get the right angle given that there’s no way to make minute adjustments. Also, it’s a bit loose on the horizontal plane i.e. moves a bit too easily. As long as the projector is not touched everything should remain in position. Some way to do minute adjustments would be nice even if they want to charge a bit more. Other than that everything is good, bumping up to 4 stars.

Low Profile, Low Price, worked well for me.

I needed a really low profile mount to fit above my ceiling fan. This worked well.That said, people need to understand how the center “shoulder” bolt works.I used some Locktite on the smaller threads that remain permanently installed in the extruded aluminum shaft.The large plastic locking ring needs to be able to travel a few turns up and down the larger diameter threads on the center bolt WITHOUT the center bolt spinning. The shoulder between the two thread sizes needs to sit tightly against the extruded aluminum post (hence the locktite).For my particular install, I had to drill a hole to get close enough to my projector’s center of mass. I also used a few 0.25” washers I had laying around to make the mounting flange level.Also, make sure you measure and understand the maximum depth you projector permits you to install mounting screws to. Mine was 7mm max, so I had to use washers to stay under that.Since there is only one locking ring to control all 6 degrees of freedom, it WILL take several tries to get it locked down and still aimed correctly.That said, good product for the price point. If you have less height restrictions than I did, a model with more individual adjustments may be easier to deal with.
