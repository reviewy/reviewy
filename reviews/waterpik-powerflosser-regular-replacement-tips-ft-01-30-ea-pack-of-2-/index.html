Product Reviews

Same item as comes with the flosser.

Was forced to search for alternatives to dental flossing when I got 9 rings put around crowns on my teeth to attach braces to. The rings will not allow dental flossing no how no way it shreds the strings into bits and hard to get any bits it holds onto back out. Anyway found this flosser system and bought one along with replacement tips here. I also picked up a package of the whitener tips. I can’t for the life of me see how it adds any whitening material to your mouth or gums or teeth. So my guess they don’t. Don’t buy the tool that costs more that comes or is sold as the whitener one at more money. The less expensive tool holds both style of tips so buy the less expensive battery tool and pick up the tips separate if you want. Otherwise after using both for about 4 months or longer now I’d recommend just these and the standard tool. They can be hard on sensitive gums and so if your braces or teeth have some issues there this might not be for you. But I find most of the time it works right between two teeth and does an excellent job of cleaning between the teeth and not too harsh being a slightly flexible but still somewhat hard plastic pick like object. I use everyday other tooth type picks too including the rubberized tipped ones that you just poke between your teeth. I really like those but they are very expensive. As are the super flat picks I also like because I can use them to help make sure my teeth are not getting deposits of calcification. Overall I would recommend this as a fairly inexpensive tool if you can’t floss normally. Some people say the tips don’t stay on the unit but there is a key to that…there is a very light indentation sort of like a tiny square mark and that goes in one position and then the tips do not come off but if you get them on upside down they will. I will run right back to good old flossing once my hardware is removed from my teeth but so grateful I found these.
