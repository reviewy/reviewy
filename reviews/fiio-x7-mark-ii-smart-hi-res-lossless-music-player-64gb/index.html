  • 64GB built-in flash storage and two micro SD card slots each capable of supporting 256GB cards

    Product Description

The X7 Mark II ushers in a new era for lossless music players with its completely redesigned internals and updated exterior. From the iconic machined volume wheel to refined surface finishes and bold sculpted lines, the X7 Mark II represents the pinnacle of FiiO’s music player design.

Take All Your Music With 64GB built-in flash storage and two micro SD card slots each capable of supporting 256GB cards, the X7 Mark II is able to hold over 570GB of music, enough for over 19,000 lossless music files. Truly, this is a player that can take your whole music collection along wherever you go.

Flagship DAC Forming the foundation of the X7 Mark II’s audio prowess is the 8-channel ESS ES9028PRO DAC, with an exceptional signal-to-noise ratio of 129dB and remarkable total harmonic distortion plus noise floor of -120dB. The result is more resolving power, better dynamics, and superior soundstage over its predecessor.

Modular Amplifier Design Shipping with the X7 Mark II is our new AM3A amplifier module, an all new design with both single-ended and balanced headphone output. Inside the AM3A is the widely acclaimed AD8620 wide bandwidth JFET op-amp, as well an OPA926 op-amp that is exclusively tailor-made for the X7 Mark II to meet the stringent requirements of FiiO Engineer’s design. Other amplifier modules are also available that can be easily swapped out to gain desired performance characteristics.

Digital to Analog Done Right Avoiding the design compromise of shared oscillators, the X7 Mark II uses three separate crystal isolators: one each for multiples of 44.1kHz and 48kHz sampling rates, as well as a dedicated oscillator for 384kHz. This design guarantees radically reduced jitter, increased clock precision and stability, and therefore more accurate signal conversion from digital to analog.

Coming from X5III, targeting balanced out

Upgraded from X5III, and the difference is great. I had the red version since it came out and decided that I want better Balanced Out performance. For the X5III, Balanced output was just an afterthought, it was underpowered and had high noise floor, bloomy bass and generally not very pleasurable to listen to. But with the AM3a included in this package, the sound is MUCH better with lower noise floor and improves upon the x5III in every aspect. With Balanced output, there is a 9db increase in separation and the soundstage is much larger. Usually the amp decides how much power it will drive into the Balanced Out and often for an amp module with both LO and BO one is always more powerful than the other. In this case with the AM3a the BO is more powerful of the two. Side note, it takes twice as power to drive the BO because it is 2 separate channels with separate ground, so it makes it just that much more complicated to do.VS the X5III, there is now dualband wifi for better HIRES streaming, the 2 slots of SD cards has faster transfer speed, the internal memory has upgraded to 64GB and that will hold maybe 15 DSD albums finally (lol). But the best and most important difference is that it’s SO MUCH FASTER. I turned off every single app on the x5 and it still stutters. I couldn’t even use some of the more popular apps because of how slow it got. With 2GBs of RAM on the X7II, now it’s faster and more fluid, easier for me to use because it can take whatever I throw at it now. You can even ROOT it to install Greenify and make your experience better. The screen of the X7II is noticeably better as well, more even and better viewing angles, no more light bleed and hot spots and colors looks much better as well.The DAC chip has been upgraded, and with Neutron running 64bit processing and Dithering on using BAL out, it’s the best I’ve heard from any Fiio device. It’s very evident Fiio has decided to keep the best for the flagship device, that way they won’t cannibalize their own sales. I have converted all my headphone cables to balanced and I’m really glad I did. I current have HD650, Xba-z5, ATH-ESW990 and few other pairs that all benefited from going to high powered Balanced.
