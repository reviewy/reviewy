GNARBOX - Portable Backup & Editing System for Any Camera, 256GB

by people ...

Product Specifications

  • Compatible with Action Cameras, DSLRs, and Drones
  • Edit and share 4K videos and RAW photos from mobile phone or tablet
  • Intel Quad-Core CPU and HD graphics GPU
  • 4GB per minute backup via USB3, SD, microSD

  • 256GB internal SanDisk Flash storage or connect your external drive

    Product Description

Size:256GB GNARBOX is a portable backup & editing system for any camera, allowing you to backup, edit and share your footage anywhere via phone app. USB3.0, microSD and SD ports allow you to connect any camera to quickly access and manage your content. Connect an external SD/HD for additional space. GNARBOX is rugged - shockproof, dustproof and ready for your adventure. Never lose your best moments again. GNARBOX has a 4000mAh battery, so you can use it for 4-6 hours on a single charge. Run out of juice? Recharge using micros. Here’s how it works: Back up 256GB of footage at up to 4GB/min to the GNARBOX, then auto-organize your footage by time, type, GPS and relevant metadata tagging. Open the GNARBOX app on your phone to start editing instantly, from 4K video to .RAW photo. Create highlight reels on the go with full creative control. Upload your media and share it with the world from the convenience of your phone. GNARBOX lets you upload in full resolution directly to your phone in 720p, 1080p, 2.7K and 4K.

Great IDEA, that WORKS could use Updates

Got this through there Kick Starter Campaign, waited over 2 years. I have been using the GNARBOX for about 3 weeks, I use it everyday…edited images in the snow in Rocky Mountain Nation Park at 12k, airports, on a bus, restaurants, clients office….does it work ? Yes… but it has quirks that the company is working on fixing…The good.Great field backup device, best I have ever used. I simply back up my memory cards to it and view all images on my ipad, RAW images work perfect… You can even do minor edits like to your photos. Save them back to your ipad and post them when you have wifi or use a cell connection on your phone. 4K videos play back smooth, again you can do minor edits, even color correct them now.The device seems water resistant when the doors are closed and it is light and compact enough. Pairing with an Ipad mini or iphone 7+ is perfect. It does heat up but really not issue as there is no physical connection. Battery last about 2-3 hours depending on what i’m doing. Certainly long enough to view what i shot and make a keeper shot list.The last update stated they are working on iphone images backups, nice but not really what I bought it for.The Bad/ or needs software fixing*SOFTWARE UPGRADES you need a cell connection, I hate this one, no way to do it over wifi, also no wifi pass through.. My ipad has no cell service* this needs to be fixed first- image rotation needs finer controls- applying luts would be nice…or some sort of general CC that can be applied to multiple images- navigation through images is cumbersome, it goes by type, date then you get the grid index of images, when you select a single images you cannot go back to the grid….- curves tool or at least histogram with levels needed- the entire interface seems like a beta, very poor scaling and not intuitive like some other ipad image editing appsHardware- One minor grip would be some way to store the cable on the device, i use tape now… My lacie backup drives have this makes travel easier knowing you didn’t forget the cableOver all I like it. GNARBOX IS NOT SAFE, IT GETS DANGEROUSLY HOT, BURNED MY HAND!

Edit: using an IR thermometer, the Gnarbox registered 149.3F. Wow.The GNARBOX burned my thumb significantly as I picked it up and moved it from my coffee table to my computer desk. After taking care of my burn, I decided to run temperature tests because I couldn’t believe how hot this thing became. I used a thermocouple to read the temperature from the device, and it’s easily in the 131+F. This is simply unacceptable, and frankly irresponsible for the company (GnarBox) to release a handheld portable product that reaches this temperature. For the record, the only task the GNARBOX was completing at the time was transfer pictures from the SD Card to the Internal Memory.Beyond the scolding issues, the functionality of the Gnarbox is significantly limited. We are in essence paying to beta test their product, a product that lacks even the most basic video and photo editing functions (like sharpness adjustments or noise reduction.) Additionally, since the internal software is so limited, you’d think they would allow you to export to Lightroom or Seedbox, sadly this is not possible.You are much better off buying the MyPassport Wireless Pro 4TB, and using Lightroom, Seedbox, or what ever app you use to edit your photos/videos.
