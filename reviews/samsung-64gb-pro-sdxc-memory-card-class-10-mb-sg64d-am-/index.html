Fastest card I’ve ever used

I bought this memory card for my canon t5i and I am really impressed at the speeds this thing is capable of. I typically go with a sandisk brand but the equivalent sandisk memory card (for speed and capacity) cost more than this card. I’ve been trying Samsung SSDs lately and haven’t had any problems so I took a chance with their memory card. It works great in my DSLR and my computer. I don’t have a memory card reader that is fast enough to really test the speeds of the card but the card is definitely not the bottle neck. I’m pretty sure the card is faster than the SD slot on my camera too. With my old card, my camera would spend a few seconds emptying the buffer after taking burst shots but with this card it clears the buffer almost immediately (taking jpg shots, not RAW or RAW + jpg). I have also taken HD movies with my camera and haven’t had any issues with the card not keeping up. The longest video I’ve taken so far was about 6 minutes and the card never skipped a beat. I imagine it is capable of recording video until either the card is full or the camera stops recording (I think the t5i only allows 30 minutes of continuous video). I would recommend this card to anyone who is looking for a fast SD card for a decent price.In order to utilize the UHS 1 class, you must have a newer card slot that can take full advantage of that speed or a USB 3.0 adapter (USB 2.0 will be too slow and will be a bottle neck if you want to use this card to its full potential). I have been using this card for about a month now and haven’t had any issues.READ MORE
