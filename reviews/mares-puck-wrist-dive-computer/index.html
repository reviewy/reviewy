Product Specifications

  • DEPTH: 492 feet
  • RGBM (Reduced Gradient Bubble Model)-algorithm
  • Full Function Air/Nitrox Dive Computer
  • Precautionary Program Settings

  • Nitrox Programmable (21-50%)

    Product Description

The new Puck series computer from Mares is perfect for those in need of pure ease. A full function RGBM dive computer with larger digits for easy reading and backlight on demand. The Mares Puck Dive Computer has a single button with easy access to smoothly scroll through the menu options. Also available in 2 gauge (style 414420) and 3 gauge (style 414421) consoles.

Great Value

I am new to diving so not sure how much this will help. I took this on 4 dives in Cozumel and it worked perfectly, I am a novice and I had not problem using this computer, and when I was done I was able to review the dives on the computer (depth, time, temp, etc.) I did not purchase the computer USB cable, now I wish I had. I was able to see the readouts perfectly underwater, and had my safety stop times. I would recomend this dive computer.

Great Economical Single or Back-up Dive Computer

Great compact dive computer. Have tested it against my Oceanic Datamax 100 on 15 dives. The Mares Puck computer was easy to use with one botton logbook scrolling and data upate. It has a better logbook feature (keeps more dives in memory with date and time of dive along with ascent rate, coldest temperature, depth and dive length. The Oceanic Datamax read 1-2 feet deeper than the Mares Puck but other data points were the same. This is a great back-up computer and/or sport diver main computer. I really like the wrist computer for ease of reading data and reducing the among of hoses I carry.

Easy Peasy: Grab and Go!

I ordered this dive computer for a backup at the last minute before a trip and it worked like a charm. If you want a basic computer that gives you the information you want to know, quickly - this is a great computer for you. I left the plastic screen protector that came with it on a whole trip (due to the reports that it scratches easy) and it still hasn’t come off. Not a scratch on it. One button does it all from depth to date, time to temp.The two features I liked best about this computer were the safety stop countdown tool and the surface time counter. Safety stop feature looks like it kicks in at exactly 20 feet for the countdown. I found that really handy, even in drifts with high surge. The surface interval timer worked like a charm too and very helpful for repetitive dives. I’ve logged 23 dives so far with it, and am very happy.

Easy, accurate

I used this for the first time in Bonaire for 15 dives. It worked great and really helped me with slower ascents (I’ve always been a little too fast and have a hard time judging this sometimes). It documented everything as the manual says. Make sure you go through the initial set up well before your first dive. I haven’t USB’d it a computer to see the additional data it records, as the cable is $75 by itself! A little ridiculous IMHO. I wish the cable would come bundled with the dive computer. USB cables should not be that expensive in this day & age. The face of the computer was easily visible in the water and easy to read. I loved how it would tell me how many minutes of dive time I had left at the current depth I was at. That helped me not stay too deep too long. I will definately be using this computer on my future dives and am actively recommending it to all my diving friends.

and glad I did

I purchased the Puck Mares as a backup dive computer to my Suunto Viper that is connected to my console on my regulator, and glad I did. While on my last dive, I discovered an air leak on the hose that connects to the BCD inflator hose, and had to use a spare regulator from the dive boat which had no computer on the console, only the cylinder psi reading and depth. The Suunto Viper has many more features, but the Puck Mares is easy to use with the one button function scrolling, and provides the basic information need such as non-deco time, accent rate times and warnings, along with max depth, water temp, and overall dive time. Although I did have to replace the battery when I first purchased it, after noticing a low battery indication. But after that, it functioned great on my last dive, and for the price did what is was designed to do, and happy with this purchase. It does have an optional USB cord, for download of dive data to your PC, but at an additional $75 cost, I’m ok with scrolling down to the logbook and writing it down on paper.
