It said that it could.So I looked at the end of the cable to see what was completing the connection for the lights to work and all it was, was solder. But the 3 wires that connect all these lights seemed bare wires and I wondered how they were working with all these wires touching each other?So I took a pair of wire cutters and snipped it to the length I needed but when I touched the wires together again, the lights wouldn’t come on! I looked at the end again that was before I cut the wires and it was just solder completing the connection. I couldn’t figure out why the lights weren’t coming on if these were bare wires!Then I thought, “what if these aren’t bare wires and there was some coating on them. So what I did was took a pair of pliers and used the “teeth” of the pliers by stripping any coating (if any) to truly make them bare wires. Sure enough, that was it. Now there are three wires to figure out which ones need to be soldered together or just twisted together (which is what I did). So if you do need to shorten the wires by cutting them, you’ll need to strip the ends as well and find out which ones need to be connected. I couldn’t find any instructions or tips that told me to do that. But they’re working great now!
