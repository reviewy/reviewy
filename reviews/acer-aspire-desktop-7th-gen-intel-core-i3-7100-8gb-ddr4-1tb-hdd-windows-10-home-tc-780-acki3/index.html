Outstanding Performance/$

When it comes to desktops, I typically prefer to build my own. However, it is not necessarily cheaper to do so. One factor is that a new license for Windows 10 starts at $100 for the Home edition. Based on reviews of other Acer Aspire desktops, I was concerned that this computer might not come with a valid, activated key for Windows, as others reported that as a common issue. Not here! Windows 10 was activated, and I even reinstalled Windows 10 on it, which activated on its own (the license key is stored on the motherboard). Pricing the other components separately: processor $115, memory $65, hard drive $40, motherboard $50, power supply $30, DVD burner $20, case $30. That’s $450 separately ($100 for Windows 10 Home), and still doesn’t include features such as wireless and bluetooth or a keyboard and mouse.While outdated, the included PowerDVD 12 was plenty adequate for the DVDs that I tested on the computer. I installed office, streamed videos, and explored the features included with Windows, all of which this computer handled without any slowdowns or other issues. I expect it to last for several years, and it can always be upgraded with a graphics card, more or faster memory, a faster processor, and/or a solid state drive. For $350 this computer is hard to beat, and the Acer brand has a reputation of quality that is displayed in the craftsmanship of this computer.
