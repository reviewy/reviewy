cheap and literally painful

There were so many great reviews on this case, I knew it had to be great too. Nope. There are only two positives to this case: the cover design (I got the mosaic…very pretty) and the plastic corners to protect from corner drops. The case does not stand up like in the image, it flops all over and is just impossible. The material all over the case, cover and back, is very thin. The biggest downfall, and you can see it if you look closely at the edges in the picture, on the three sides of the cover, there is no cardboard. The plastic comes together sharply on the edges and downright hurt my hand when I held it with the cover flipped behind the tablet. I had to take it off. Since the material is so flimsy, you never have a chance of the cover lining up with the back of the case, part of it is always falling out of alignment.I bought a Moko case instead and am much happier with the product. I have no idea why fintie cases are the rage, but don’t waste your money.

Nice case, iffy auto wake

This case is quite great. I like the thin form fit of the case around the tablet. My only gripe is that the auto wake function is totally hit or miss. Sometimes it works, most of the time it does not. I’d have to trace it back to the magnets on the case not being flat on the surface when latching onto the cover flap. With these kinds of cases, every millimeter matters, and a tiny flaw like that can make it not work exactly as advertised.I’m only dinging a star off for that because it otherwise does its intended purpose well, which is to protect my Galaxy Tab A.
