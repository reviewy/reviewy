It’s fine

I wouldn’t call this “heavy duty” but it does the job. Took about 10 minutes to set up and it probably would have taken less if the pieces that hold the shelves up fit together better. Most of them didn’t close around the pole and I had to use a hammer to tap the shelving down over the pieces to make sure they would hold. That being said, it holds all the stuff I wanted it to hold and that’s what’s important. It’s not completely level but the bottom of the poles have screws so you can adjust how it stands. Worth it for the price paid and it was undamaged by shipping.

You almost get what you pay for

I’ve bought the original Metro shelves and the Ikea equivalent for years. Metro are, without a doubt, the most durable, but also more expensive. This Furinno was broken right out of the box as the welds on one of the shelves were already broken. I am using that shelf on the top and putting nothing on it. As for the 250 pounds per shelf? No. I have about 50 on one of them and it is starting to bow. Perhaps if the 250 is spread out with the bulk at the sides of the shelves, it could work, but I wouldn’t recommend it. As well, the plastic sleeves that are used to hold the shelves in place are about 10% too small as they don’t snap into each other as they should.I like the green color and it’s being used until we find something more permanent, but if you want this to be a heavy duty and long lasting solution, look to Metro or something else. It’s not terrible, but it’s not good.
